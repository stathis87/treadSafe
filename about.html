Artists

Ieva Vaiti

Ieva is a musician and artist working with technology to produce works in the realm of print, installation and performance. Her background in music has taken her to working as a sound designer and composer for film & media; recreating these sounds in post-production houses for giants such as Disney, BBC and so on. However her interests lie in more than solely the aesthetics of our environment - but more of what it’s made of. By using technology she brings forth the abstractions of matter in ways that resemble analog forms resulting in visual art, tools and interactive experiences.

Maria Gracia Cebrecos

Maria Gracia is a Peruvian artist, architect, photographer and mainly storyteller whose work explores themes of origin, memory, and identity. Her practice is tied to the land, beliefs, history, mythology, and the presence of bodies in space. She is interested in the relationship between reality, fiction and the power of our senses to understand the world. Because of her background and passion in architecture and photography, she tells stories through space, creating installations that combine visual and interactive narratives.

Ewan Peng

Ewan is a Chinese artist, currently based in London. His research focuses on the intersection of art, sound and technology, exploring themes of cybernetics, body, and sociology. Since three years ago, he has been actively engaged in exploring the possibilities of computational arts, and intends to continue his exploration in this field in the future.

with-lasers

Nathan is a computational artist practicing under the name with-lasers. This practice, which explores ideas of the artists subconscious and concious worlds is primarily driven by looking to the history of Computational Art, and tracing paths not taken, by applying the power of today’s technology to ideas previously constrained - by this very act of re-imagining and updating, new possibilities and ways of developing material are opened, allowing for new modes of expression.

Robin Leverton

Robin Leverton investigates systemic collapse through installation and performance. Utilising robotics, machine learning and computer vision, he constructs dynamic relationships between audiences and their environment to question their role in its collapse. Through this lens he seeks to develop analogies to the fractal self-similar systems that govern our societies and environment. By creating systems that are designed to fail, he aim to draw attention to the compulsory participation within a global economy that is destroying both our planet and our societies.

Stathis Doganis

Stathis Doganis is a Greek artist. His practice is in between media and performance and recently he started experimenting with computational media as a means of developing new forms of dramaturgy in his work. He has collaborated with various artists in creating works such as “Sans Attente” (2013), “Breathe” (2015), “Under Siege” (2016), “Matter” (2017), “in relation” (2016), “Reflections” (2017), “Silence” (2018) and “Pose_Transpose (2021).

In parallel with his artistic practice, he works as a museum professional; He designs and develops intuitive digital productions to foster visitors’ interactivity and enhance their experience.

Kruti Shah

I am an Architect who likes to explore the intersection between Art, Architecture and technology. Light , spaces and interactive art are the mediums I like to work with in my practice. I am more of a fabricator who likes to create, experiment and destroy things. I also try to inculcate society , culture and nature into my art piece which questions reality like in one of my project ‘Her limits’ which talks about the struggles of women in the 21st century. Technology in art gives me more freedom to explore the spaces which are more fluid in nature which responds to the environment that surrounds it. I aim to experiment with more site specific interactive installation as every space has a story to tell.

Millieon Hu

Millieon is an artist and data scientist from Beijing. She explores artistic ways of seeing and analyse data through different perspectives, sounds and visuals. Her works touches on the topics of nature and simulation, diaspora, queerness, censorship, and the social construction of ideologies.

Sofia Taipa

Originally from Lisbon, Sofia is a computational artist based in London, with a background in both arts and computer science. Her interests include interactive art, creative coding and web development. Through computation and installation, Sofia researches the overarching fields of perception and intermedia, exploring the intersection between different sensing experiences.
