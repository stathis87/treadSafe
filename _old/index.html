SOME SILVER BURNS

Ieva Vaiti

Performance

Some Silver Burns is a multimedia contemporary dance performance shown at The Place, London in February 2020. Touching on themes of fragility and loss, the piece reflects on our journey to healing in four movements. I co-directed the piece with my creative partner Amy Dang and composed the music.
Animation: Amy Dang / Choreography: Magnus Westwell /
Dancers: Olivia Grassot & Oscar Jinghuli / Costumes: Khanh Brice-Nguyen

Phase of the project: Completed

MORPHED MEMORIES

Maria Gracia Cebrecos

Photography & interactive installation

We all experience some form of dementia in our lives. This room contains some of “his” memories, brought to life by the presence of visitors. The memories reveal themselves, but also fade away unless there is a human presence to perceive them. Using his archival photographs and real time camera projections....(continue). The installation creates a living room scenery, the place where memories are collected and experiences are shared. With three different sets of interactions based on proximity and play, visitors can immerse themselves in the memories of the room.

Phase of the project: Ongoing

NAUSEA

Ewan Peng

Physical computing

This is a cyclic vomiting machine which has a mouth, two lungs, and some weird sounds. The machine can only do one thing, which is puke the blood again and again. It has a plastic lung to store the blood, a pressure sensor to control the sound when the lung moves, and a silicone mouth for puke.

Phase of the project: Completed

ALEPH-NULL

with-lasers

Audio-Visual Performance & Real Time Graphics

Continues the artists work and research, which originated in the work of John and James Whitney. By reinterpreting the physical techniques, supported by early computing technology used by them, and making use of the technology available in the 21st century, new forms and directions can be explored, tracing paths previously not taken. By using this underlying mathematical model to create both visuals and sound, the real time performance is a meditation and exploration of the unconscious, and its continued influence in our everyday activities.

Phase of the project: Completed

MACHINE DRAWING: SPACE AND TIME

Robin Leverton

Drawing & Physical computing

Space and Time is an installation which utilises a custom-made pen-plotter to continuously draw the space it faces. The work continues my interests in systemic collapse and de-centralised responsibility, whereby through intended interaction with the system, the participant is partially responsible for its ultimate failure. In respect to Machine Drawing: Space and Time this is represented by the continuous redrawing over the same page; A space’s architecture may be neatly re-constructed, but its living occupants create a dynamic subject that changes with each frame taken by the machine’s camera....

Phase of the project: Ongoing

POSE_TRANSPOSE

Stathis Doganis

Website, video dance & interactive map

“Pose_Transpose” is at once a performance in public and private space, a film shoot, a film, and a website hosting an utterly idiosyncratic map of the city.The work renders an experiential observation of the events that unfolded in quarantine in 2020– 2021. A map of movement traces has been created with a view to juxtaposing recent experiences with the limitations imposed on freedom of movement.
Concept, choreography, direction & text: Stathis Doganis, Myrto Delimichali / Performers: Antonis Antoniou, Maria Vourou, Myrto Delimichali, Stathis Doganis, Loukiani Papadaki, Thanos Ragkousis, Despina Sanida-Krezia, Fotini Stamatelopoulou, Despina Stasinou / Visual performance (mask construction): Antonis Antoniou / Music composition: Rafailia Bampasidou / Filming: Stathis Doganis, Dimitris Tranos / Audio mastering: Michalis Ikonomidis

Phase of the project: Completed

HER LIMITS

Kruti Shah

Sculpture & Physical computing

It is a wooden kinetic sculpture with motorized hands which signifies Household activities, Money, Beauty and childcare. The four major responsibilities society expects from women in the 21st century. By using these robotic hands we wanted to disembody the women from her duties, thus freeing her of the labour she endures. The vibrant color signifies the invisible effort that she is making on a daily basis.

Phase of the project: Completed

Space Colony

Millieon Hu

Visual projection & Computer Vision

This is a simulation of ant colony behaviour, a multi-agent system where the agents search for food, leave pheromones and die. This project uses computer vision to find the "food" in real life; the virtual ants search for the real food that exists in our physical space and gradually form a trail around the food. This work meant to break the boundary between the physical and virtual reality.

Phase of the project: Completed

Synesthesia

Sofia Taipa

Interactive installation & Physical computing

Synesthesia immerses the audience in instances of chaos and harmony. It is an interactive experience on the visualization of pure frequencies, focusing on how these can be perceived in an unconventional manner. The movement induced by the sound waves creates intricate objects with a three-dimensional nature. It investigates the threshold where sound corrugates into image - the moment it becomes visually perceptible. What was once incorporeal becomes almost tangible.

Phase of the project: Ongoing
